Perfect Joints
**************

One of the issues you end up dealing with in designing with OpenSCAD is that
sometimes it is just too precise. Look closely at a typical dihedral joint. 

We start off with a flat wing, perhaps constructed in one assembly. The leading
and trailing edges are nice and straight. So, we take a knife and make cuts
where the dihedral break should go. Some folks cut squarely at the inner rib,
others like to slice the spar at an angle outside of the rib.
